Core Finding

Both crises share the same fundamental cause: loss of confidence triggering self-reinforcing runs. The critical difference lies in institutional design—the presence or absence of circuit breakers, regulatory oversight, and lender-of-last-resort facilities determines whether a run is contained or catastrophic.

A.2: Where Does the Money Go?

Terra-Luna 2022

Flight FROM:

  • UST (algorithmic) — complete exodus
  • LUNA — hyperinflationary collapse

Flight TO:

  • USDC — Circle-audited reserves
  • USDT — largest market cap
  • DAI — over-collateralized
  • Fiat exits via exchanges
Insight: Capital fled from algorithmic to asset-backed stablecoins—investors implicitly understood collateral matters.

GFC 2008

Flight FROM:

  • Prime MMFs — CP exposure
  • Financial stocks (AIG, Citi, Lehman)

Flight TO:

  • Treasury bills — yields near 0%
  • Government MMFs — full backing
  • FDIC-insured deposits
Insight: Capital fled to government-backed assets—regulatory backstops are the ultimate safe haven.

Common Pattern

In both crises, capital fled from promises of stability to actual backing—whether asset reserves (USDC) or government guarantees (Treasuries).

Institutional Design: Why Structure Matters

Feature Terra-Luna (2022) Reserve Primary Fund (2008)
Backing Mechanism Algorithmic (LUNA arbitrage) Asset-backed (CP, Treasuries)
Trading Hours 24/7 globally US market hours only
Lender of Last Resort None Federal Reserve
Deposit Insurance None Treasury Guarantee Program
Circuit Breakers None SEC/Exchange halts
Regulatory Oversight Minimal SEC, FINRA regulated
Investor Recovery ~0% ~99 cents/dollar
Conclusion: Terra failed in every dimension where traditional finance provided protection. The lesson is not that stablecoins are inherently flawed, but that institutional design determines outcomes.

Section B: Policy Recommendation

Proposed: Panic-Index-Triggered Circuit Breakers + Over-Collateralization

1. Real-Time Panic Index Monitoring

On-chain oracles compute Panic Index hourly. Thresholds trigger automatic responses:

Panic LevelAction
<2 (Normal)No restrictions
2-5 (Elevated)Warning alerts
5-7 (High)5% daily redemption cap
>7 (Critical)24-hour redemption halt

2. 150% Over-Collateralization

Reserves must exceed outstanding stablecoins by 50%, providing buffer against volatility.

Why This Works

Our Panic Index detected crisis 6 days early. A circuit breaker at level 5 would have triggered on May 7—before the death spiral accelerated.

Trade-offs Acknowledged

Cost: Over-collateralization reduces capital efficiency (150% vs 0% for pure algorithmic).

Benefit: Prevents 100% loss scenarios; provides intervention window.

Philosophy: Import stability mechanisms from TradFi while preserving decentralization benefits.

Conclusion: Lessons for Financial Stability

Key Findings

  1. Same Cause, Different Outcomes: Both crises stemmed from confidence loss, but institutional design determined severity.
  2. Speed Matters: 72-hour collapses require pre-built safeguards—human intervention cannot keep pace.
  3. Transparency is Double-Edged: Real-time data can fuel panic as easily as it builds trust.
  4. Backing Beats Algorithms: Capital flees to actual reserves, not mathematical promises.

Novel Contributions

1. Panic Index: Real-time crisis detection metric derived from on-chain data (peak: 7.59)

2. Early Warning: Detected crisis 6 days before price collapse

3. Contagion Quantification: UST-USDC correlation jumped 3.7x during crisis

4. Capital Flight Forensics: Whales exited before retail (information asymmetry)

Final Thought: The question is not whether crypto can be stable—it's whether we will build the institutions to make stability possible.
